Navigation Menu

Textile Museum of Canada
Read Project

Goal: Redesign the IA and navigation menu for the Textile Museum of Canada to improve content discoverability.


Requirements: The IA must improve ease of use and content discoverability and serve their two target demographics - visitors and donors. 

 

Deliverable: Redesigned IA and menu design.​

​

Completed as a group project as part of the course INF2170 (Information Architecture) at the Faculty of Information, University of Toronto.

Role

I was responsible for specific tasks throughout the project.

Content Inventory and Audit

We audited the content of 32 pages, and evaluated the pages. I did 8 pages.

IA UX research

We did Card Sorting and Tree Testing with 2 participants each.

Graphic Design

Ideation and Design of Navigation Menu. I did the 2 ideas (short and long term) that were finalized.

Challenge

How can we improve discoverability of content and wayfinding across the website?

This project focused on redesigning the museum’s information architecture and navigation to effectively communicate its core values and present information in a clear, accessible manner for its target audiences.​

The IA needed to be redone and presented differently.

Content Inventory & Audit

Content Audit outcomes

In assessing the collection of pages we utilized four criteria: Audience, User Expectation, Recency & Relevancy, and Call To Actions in order to prescribe meaning to the content within our interpretation. 
 

These four criteria were chosen due to their ability to describe a piece of content’s worth and value to the website, and to users browsing the site. 

​

We evaluated 32 pages within the Textile Museum website and found that while most of the content was informative, and up to date, there were issues in the organization of information and the consistency of titles kept through the website. 

Core Issue
Explanation
No Clear Call to Action for "Visit" Page

While the "Visit" page provides essential information about tickets, guidelines, and directions, there is no clear call to action, such as a prominent button or link, encouraging visitors to take specific actions like booking tickets or exploring membership options.

No Differentiation in Internship Application Process

The internship page does not distinguish between paid and unpaid opportunities or provide specific details about each type. This lack of clarity may impact individuals interested in internships.

Limited Details on Job and Internship Opportunities

The pages dedicated to job and internship opportunities lack comprehensive information about positions, benefits, and the work environment. This may deter potential applicants from understanding the full scope of available opportunities.

Incomplete Information on Volunteer Positions

Pages describing specific volunteer opportunities provide some information about roles but lack details on whether volunteers are actively being hired, specific requirements, and benefits. This could hinder potential volunteers in making informed decisions.

Lack of Recency Indicators

Many pages, particularly those related to health and safety guidelines, since covid lack a clear indication of if  their information is still relevant. This is due to the url containing the information “covid-19” and “october. Volunteering opportunities, lack clear indications of last updates, and if they are currently accepting volunteers.

Current Menu

The current menu is hidden away inside a hamburger menu, it isn't less-complex nor it is accessible.

​

The menu covers the entire page and there is no indication as to what page the user is in nor does it have a way for the user to go home.

​

The menu also uses the + symbol to reveal the sub menu, which is not intuitive and might lead to mis-clicks as the menu items themselves are pages, not dropdown folders.

Conclusion

Concluding thoughts and Takeaways

The goal of this project was to redesign the navigation for the Textile Museum of Canada, making it easier for users to find information without feeling lost.
​
The biggest challenge was organizing the content in a way that made sense for different types of visitors while keeping the design simple and functional. By restructuring the menu and improving labels, users can now access key sections like exhibitions, collections, and resources more easily.

Did I do something new?

This was the first time I had tried my hand at understanding and solving for one specific feature of a product instead of the product as a whole. As this was a course project, I had to work with a team of 3 equals and I learnt how to collaborate, a welcome change as I was used to working as a team of one.
